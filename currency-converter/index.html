Convert any money in seconds with this live currency converter

This free currency converter turns one amount into its value in another currency using up-to-date exchange rates for more than 150 of the world's currencies. You type a number, choose the currency you are starting from and the one you want to end up in, and the converted figure appears straight away — along with the exact rate behind it. There is nothing to install, no account to create, and no limit on how many conversions you can run.

It is built for everyday situations where you need a quick, honest number rather than a bank quote. Travellers checking a hotel price abroad, online shoppers comparing a listing in a foreign store, freelancers invoicing overseas clients, families sending or receiving money, and students reading prices in another country all benefit from seeing a clear figure in their own currency. Popular local pairs such as USD to PKR, GBP to PKR, USD to INR and AED to PKR are one tap away, so you rarely have to scroll through the full list.

How to use it

  1. Type the amount you want to convert into the first box.
  2. Pick the currency you are converting from using the first dropdown.
  3. Pick the currency you want the result in using the second dropdown.
  4. Read the converted amount instantly — the panel also shows the live rate in both directions.
  5. Use the round swap button to flip the two currencies, or tap a popular pair chip for a one-click shortcut.

What an exchange rate actually means

An exchange rate is simply the price of one currency expressed in another. If the rate for 1 US Dollar is 278 Pakistani Rupees, then converting any USD amount means multiplying it by 278, and converting back from PKR means dividing by the same number. This converter does that maths for you and shows you both views at once: how much one unit of the source currency is worth in the target currency, and the reverse. Seeing both directions helps you sanity-check a price quickly — if a number looks too large or too small, the per-unit rate makes the mistake obvious. The result is also formatted with thousands separators and sensible decimal places, switching to extra decimals for very small per-unit values so tiny rates stay readable.

Where the rates come from and how fresh they are

Rates are fetched live from a public exchange-rate feed each time you open the page, and the panel shows a small status indicator with the date they were last updated. A green dot means you are viewing fresh live rates; if the network is unavailable, the converter falls back to the last set of rates it saved on your device and the dot turns red so you know the figures may be slightly stale. These are mid-market reference rates — the midpoint between buy and sell prices — which makes them ideal for comparison and budgeting. They are not the exact rate a bank, card network or money-transfer service will give you, because those providers add a margin or fee on top. Treat the result as a reliable guide rather than a transactional quote.

Tips, common mistakes and accuracy notes

A few habits make the tool more useful. Double-check the direction before you trust a figure: converting USD to PKR and PKR to USD give very different numbers, and the swap button exists precisely so you can flip them without retyping. When comparing a foreign price, convert the total you will actually pay, including taxes or shipping, rather than the headline figure. Remember that the rate you see here will not include the spread your provider charges, so for an international payment, budget a little extra. Currencies with very large unit counts, such as the Indonesian Rupiah or some others, can produce big-looking numbers — that is normal, not an error. Because rates move throughout the day, a conversion is a snapshot of the moment you ran it; refresh the page before making an important decision.

Privacy and offline use

Your amounts and currency choices never leave your browser — the only network request is the one that downloads the public list of rates, and nothing you type is uploaded, logged or shared. The last rates are stored locally on your own device so the converter still works if you briefly lose connection, which is handy when you are travelling on patchy mobile data. No signup, no cookies for your inputs, and no tracking of what you convert.

Exchange rates are mid-market reference values for information only and may differ from the rate your bank or provider uses.

FAQ

Is this currency converter free?

Yes, it is completely free with no signup, no app to download and no limit on conversions. Open the page and start converting immediately.

How often are the exchange rates updated?

Rates are fetched live from a public exchange-rate feed each time you load the page, and they refresh at least once a day at the source. The panel shows the date the rates were last updated so you always know how fresh they are.

Which currencies are supported?

More than 150 currencies are covered, including USD, EUR, GBP, PKR, INR, AED, SAR, JPY, CNY, CAD, AUD and many more. The most-used currencies are grouped at the top of each dropdown for quick access.

Are these the same rates my bank or money-transfer service will give me?

Not exactly. The figures here are mid-market reference rates — the midpoint between buy and sell prices — so they are great for comparing and budgeting. Banks, card networks and transfer services usually add a margin or fee, so your final amount may be a little lower. Use this as a reliable guide rather than a transactional quote.

Does it work offline?

Partly. The converter saves the last set of rates on your device, so if you lose connection it keeps working using those saved figures. The status dot turns red to tell you the rates may be slightly out of date until you are back online and can refresh.

Is my data private?

Yes. The amounts and currencies you choose stay in your browser and are never uploaded or shared. The only network request downloads the public rate list; nothing you type is sent anywhere.
