Frequently Asked Questions

What is BMI?

BMI (Body Mass Index) is a simple measure using your height and weight to estimate body fat. The formula is weight in kg divided by height in meters squared. It's widely used as a quick health screening tool.

What is a healthy BMI range?

A healthy BMI is between 18.5 and 24.9. Below 18.5 is underweight, 25-29.9 is overweight, and 30 or above is considered obese. These ranges apply to most adults.

Is BMI accurate for athletes?

BMI doesn't distinguish between muscle and fat. Athletes with high muscle mass may show a high BMI despite being healthy. For a complete assessment, consult a healthcare professional who can measure body composition directly.

Does age or gender affect BMI?

The BMI formula is the same for all adults. However, women typically carry more body fat than men at the same BMI, and older adults tend to have more body fat than younger adults at equal BMI levels.
