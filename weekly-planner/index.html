A free weekly planner that actually feels good to use

Most weekly planners are either a paid spreadsheet or a cluttered app that wants your email first. This one is different: open it, type your tasks under each day, and tick them off as you go. Your plan saves automatically in your browser, so it's still here when you come back — no account, no signup, no cost.

Each day has its own progress ring that fills as you complete tasks, and the ring at the top shows how your whole week is going at a glance. It turns a boring to-do list into something you actually want to finish. Pick a colour theme that matches your mood, and make it yours.

How to use the weekly planner

  1. Give your week a title and an optional focus or goal.
  2. Type a task under any day and press Enter (or tap +) to add it.
  3. Tap the circle to mark a task done — the rings update instantly.
  4. Star important tasks so they stand out.
  5. Download your plan as an image to share, or print it for your desk.
  6. Hit New week to uncheck everything and start fresh, keeping your routine.

Is this weekly planner free?

Yes — completely free, with no signup, no watermark and no limits. Use it every single week.

Will my tasks be saved?

Yes. Everything is stored locally in your browser, so your plan stays on this device even after you close the tab. Nothing is ever uploaded to a server.

Can I download or print it?

Yes. Download a high-resolution image to share or use as a phone wallpaper, or print the planner directly from the page.
