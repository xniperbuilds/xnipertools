A free all-in-one school toolkit that does a teacher's paperwork in minutes

Most classroom paperwork still gets built by hand in Word, where one stray tab shifts a whole table, marks never add up, and Urdu text breaks the moment you print. This free school tools suite replaces that struggle with twelve focused makers under one roof: a test and exam paper generator, an MCQ worksheet maker, an OMR bubble answer sheet generator, a report card maker, a lesson planner, an exam date sheet maker, a monthly attendance register, an admit card and roll number slip maker, a class timetable builder, a formal application writer, a certificate maker, and a student ID card generator. You fill in plain fields, a live preview redraws as you type, and you print or save a clean PDF when it looks right.

It is built for the teacher, head teacher, coaching-centre tutor or school clerk who has to produce these documents week after week and does not want to fight software to do it. Every tool works in English, Urdu and Arabic, flips the page to right-to-left automatically for the last two, and offers a colourful template plus an ink-saving black-and-white version for the photocopier. Nothing needs installing and there is no account to create — open the page, pick a tool from the tab bar, and you are working.

How to use it

  1. Pick a tool from the row of tabs at the top — for example Test / Exam Paper, Report Card or ID Card.
  2. Fill in the shared header once: school name, logo, class, section, subject and exam name. It carries across the tools that need it.
  3. Add your content in the control panel on the left — questions and marks, subjects and scores, periods, names or card details depending on the tool.
  4. Choose a template, set the language to English, Urdu or Arabic, and flip black-and-white on if you are photocopying.
  5. Watch the white preview on the right update instantly, then click Print to save as PDF from the browser dialog, or Download PDF for a file.

What each of the twelve tools actually does

The paper generator handles real exam structure: separate sections for multiple-choice, short, long, fill-in-the-blank and true/false questions, each with its own marks, plus an instructions box and signature lines. The MCQ sheet maker lays out four options per question and can print a separate answer key for fast marking. The OMR generator draws a proper bubble answer sheet with a roll-number grid for any number of questions. The report card tool is the one that saves the most time — type each subject's total and obtained marks and it auto-calculates the grand total, percentage, grade and pass or fail result, so you never reach for a calculator. The rest cover the everyday rather than the exam hall: lesson plans with objectives and homework, exam date sheets, monthly attendance registers, admit cards and roll-number slips, weekly class timetables, formal applications (leave, fee concession, character certificate and more), certificates of achievement, and CR80-size student ID cards with a photo, QR code, four designs and batch printing for a whole class.

Why right-to-left and black-and-white actually matter

Urdu and Arabic are not just a font swap. When you choose either language the whole page switches to right-to-left flow, loads the correct Nastaliq or Naskh script, and translates the field labels — Name, Roll No, Total Marks, Time and the rest — so the document reads naturally to a parent or examiner. That is why these languages print cleanest through the browser's own Print-to-PDF rather than an image-based download. The black-and-white toggle is the other quiet money-saver: it strips coloured header bands and tints down to clean lines and grey shading, so a class set runs through a photocopier without draining a colour cartridge. Both options exist on every tool, not just the paper maker.

Your data never leaves your computer

Everything here runs entirely inside your browser. The names, marks, photos and logos you enter are processed on your own device and are never uploaded to a server — there is no sign-up, no cloud storage and no copy kept of a student's details. Because the work is local it also stays fast and keeps working even on a patchy school connection. When you are done, the only thing that leaves the page is the PDF you choose to download. For the sharpest output, especially with photos on ID cards or Urdu and Arabic text, use Print and Save as PDF; Download PDF is the quick one-click option when you just need a file in a hurry.

FAQ

What can I make with these school tools?

Twelve tools in one place: exam and test papers, MCQ worksheets with an answer key, OMR bubble answer sheets, student report cards with auto-calculated grades, lesson plans, exam date sheets, monthly attendance registers, admit cards and roll number slips, class timetables, formal applications, certificates, and student ID cards with photos and QR codes. Each one has several templates.

Can I make papers in Urdu or Arabic?

Yes. Switch the language to Urdu or Arabic and the whole page flows right to left with the correct Nastaliq or Naskh script. Field labels like Name, Roll No, Total Marks and Time are translated for you, so the document reads naturally to parents and examiners. It works on every tool, not only the paper maker.

How do I get a PDF?

Click Print to save directly as a PDF from your browser's print dialog, or click Download PDF to get a file in one step. You can choose a colourful template or a clean black-and-white version that saves ink on the photocopier. For Urdu, Arabic and ID-card photos, Print and Save as PDF gives the sharpest result.

Does the report card calculate grades and percentages for me?

Yes. In the Report Card tool you just type each subject's total and obtained marks. It adds the grand total, works out the percentage, assigns the grade and shows whether the student passed or failed automatically — no calculator needed, and the totals update the moment you change a number.

Can I make ID cards for a whole class at once?

Yes. The ID Card tool prints at real CR80 card size and lets you add multiple students with a photo and QR code each, choose from four designs, and batch print the whole class together. The shared school name, logo and address fill in once and apply to every card.

Is it free, and is my data safe?

Every tool here is 100% free with no signup or watermark. Everything runs inside your browser — the names, marks, photos and logos you enter stay on your own device and are never uploaded to any server. The only thing that leaves the page is the PDF you choose to download.
