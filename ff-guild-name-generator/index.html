Guild names that fit Free Fire's 12-character limit

Naming a Free Fire guild is harder than naming yourself: the guild field allows only 12 characters, every stylish symbol counts toward that cap, and the good short names on your server go fast. This guild name generator is built around that exact constraint. Type a name to see it styled with fonts and guild marks like 亗 and ༒, or generate fresh ideas from scratch — and every single result carries a live green/red counter, so you know a name fits before you spend diamonds on it.

Use it when you are creating a new guild, rebranding an old one, or matching your guild name to your squad's clan tags. Because the names are ordinary Unicode text, they paste cleanly into the guild creation screen, and they work just as well for clans in PUBG Mobile, BGMI and Call of Duty Mobile. Everything runs in your browser — nothing you type leaves your device.

How to use it

  1. Type your guild name idea (up to 12 characters) and tap Style it to see decorated versions.
  2. No idea yet? Tap Generate ideas for fresh names like DARKLEGION, 亗IRONWOLVES and ༒PHANTOMS༒.
  3. Browse the ready guild names by vibe — attitude, pro/esports, symbol-heavy or clean one-word names.
  4. Watch the counter: green means the name fits the 12-character guild limit, red means trim it.
  5. Tap Copy, then in Free Fire open the Guild tab → Create Guild (or rename in guild settings) and paste.

What makes a good guild name

The best guild names are short, aggressive and readable at a glance in the lobby and on the guild leaderboard. One strong word — IMMORTALS, WARLORDS, PHANTOMS — usually beats two weak ones, and it leaves budget for a single mark like 亗 or ༒ at the front. If your members use clan tags in their nicknames, echo the guild name in the tag (guild DARKLEGION → tag DL) so the whole roster reads as one team. Avoid names that only differ from a famous guild by one letter; they read as knock-offs and are often already taken on busy servers.

Creating and renaming guilds costs diamonds — check first

Creating a guild costs 1000 diamonds (or 5000 guild tokens), and renaming an existing guild costs another 500, so a name mistake is expensive. That is exactly why every result here shows its true character count the way the game measures it. Symbols are the usual trap: a wing like ᭄ or a bracket pair like ꧁꧂ can quietly push a 10-letter name past the cap. Paste your final choice into the guild screen and confirm the preview looks right before you pay.

Guild name + matching player names

A coordinated look sells a serious guild. Once your guild name is set, send your members to the Free Fire name generator to build matching nicknames — same clan tag, same separator, same font family. The pro-player styles there (Raistar, SK Sabir Boss, White444) also work well as a shared roster style under one guild banner.

FAQ

What is the Free Fire guild name character limit?

A Free Fire guild name can be at most 12 characters, and every symbol or decorated letter counts toward that cap. Each name on this page shows a live counter like 10/12 — green fits, red is too long.

How do I create or rename a guild in Free Fire?

Open the Guild tab in Free Fire and tap Create Guild, then paste the name you copied here. Creating a guild costs 1000 diamonds or 5000 guild tokens; renaming an existing guild costs 500 diamonds via the guild settings.

Can I use symbols like 亗 and ༒ in a guild name?

Yes. Guild names accept the same Unicode symbols as nicknames, so 亗, ༒, メ, ꧁꧂ and similar marks work. Just remember each symbol takes one or two characters of the 12-character budget, which the counter on this page tracks for you.

Why was my guild name rejected in-game?

The usual reasons are going over 12 characters, using a name another guild already registered on your server, or including words Garena's filter blocks. If a name here shows green but is rejected, it is almost always already taken — try a variation or different symbols.

Do these guild names work in PUBG, BGMI and other games?

Yes. The names are plain Unicode text, so they work for clans and crews in PUBG Mobile, BGMI, Call of Duty Mobile and most other games — though each game has its own length limit, so check before pasting.

Do you store the guild names I create?

No. Everything runs in your browser — nothing you type is sent to a server, and favourites are stored only on your own device using local storage.
